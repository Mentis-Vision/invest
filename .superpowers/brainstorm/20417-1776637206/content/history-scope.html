What is "History" actually for?

Today it's a mashup: every research query + every recommendation + the few actions you've marked. That's why you got a "recommendation on a stock you don't own" — Research on NVDA-out-of-curiosity landed in the same pile as "LINK is 51% of your portfolio." Three ways to untangle it:

A

Rename to "Journal" — actions only

The page shows only rows you've taken an action on (Took / Partial / Ignored / Opposed). If you dismissed the Strategy move on LINK, it shows up here. If you researched NVDA for fun and never acted, it does NOT show up here — it lives on the Research page as a small "Recent searches" strip.

Pros: Crystal-clear purpose. Everything in the journal is signal. Pattern insights + reflection prompts get sharper. Cons: "Where did that NVDA research go?" → answer: Research page's Recent strip. Need a quick link to find it.

B

Two tabs on the same page: "Actions" and "Research log"

Keep the page named "Track Record" or similar. Two tabs inside: one for actions (journal), one for every ticker you've researched whether you acted on it or not. User chooses the lens.

Pros: Everything in one place. Power users like the tab separation. Cons: Two mental models under one label. Default tab becomes a bikeshed.

C

Keep everything mixed, fix labels per-row

One flat list, every row clearly marked — "Research (unacted)", "Action: Took", "Strategy move: Dismissed". User filters with the existing chip bar.

Pros: Minimum churn to the data model. Cons: Same fundamental confusion as today — page still has no single purpose.

My recommendation: A (Journal). Your concern was "a recommendation on a stock I don't own showed up" — that's because Research queries and Actions share a surface. Splitting them cleanly fixes the "this shouldn't be here" feeling AND gives us sharper pattern insights (we stop averaging "things you looked at" with "things you actually did").
