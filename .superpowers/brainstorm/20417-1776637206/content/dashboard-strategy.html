Where should the "next move" live?

Under the A+B pattern you picked — morning briefing + recommended action — we have to decide how the Dashboard and Strategy page relate. Three options:

A

Integrated — Dashboard IS the decision surface

"Next move" becomes a prominent card at the top of the Dashboard. Big, clickable, with the Done / Snooze / Dismiss chips. Strategy tab goes away. The deep multi-lens analysis is still there but expands inline ("See the full brief ↓") rather than living on a separate page.

Pros: 90-second daily loop. One page to open. No "where is it?" Cons: Dashboard gets denser; power users lose a dedicated deep-read page.

B

Linked — Dashboard previews, Strategy is the deep dive

Dashboard shows a compact Next Move tile ("Trim LINK to 25% →"). Click opens the full Strategy page with lens panels, red flags, agreed points, etc. Both pages exist; Dashboard is the briefing, Strategy is the evidence locker.

Pros: Clean separation. Power users still have Strategy. Cons: Two surfaces to maintain. More nav confusion — the problem we're solving.

C

Duplicated — Next Move on both, same data

Same Next Move card lives on Dashboard AND Strategy, with identical chips. Acting from either surface persists the same state. Strategy adds the deeper context cards below.

Pros: No wrong place to act. Cons: Two copies of the same UI — diverges over time, users unsure which is "canonical."

My recommendation: A (Integrated). Under A+B core purpose, Strategy-as-separate-page is the vestigial organ. The briefing IS the decision. Consolidating also cleans up three of your five tabs. But the tradeoff is real if you have users who want a long-form read — tell me if that's common.
